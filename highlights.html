About Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. It commemorates the first revelation of the Quran to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

Hajj & Umrah Guide

Complete step-by-step guide for performing Hajj and Umrah

Hajj Steps (Pillar by Pillar)

  • 1 Ihram - Enter the state of Ihram at the designated Miqat point. Make intention for Hajj and recite Talbiyah.
  • 2 Tawaf - Perform seven circuits around the Kaaba, starting and ending at the Black Stone (Hajar al-Aswad).
  • 3 Sa'i - Walk seven times between Safa and Marwah hills, commemorating Hajar's search for water.
  • 4 Wuquf at Arafat - Stand in prayer and supplication at the plain of Arafat on the 9th of Dhul-Hijjah.
  • 5 Muzdalifah - Spend the night at Muzdalifah and collect pebbles for the stoning ritual.
  • 6 Ramy al-Jamarat - Stone the three pillars representing Satan in Mina on the 10th, 11th, and 12th of Dhul-Hijjah.
  • 7 Sacrifice (Qurbani) - Offer an animal sacrifice or have it done on your behalf.
  • 8 Halq or Taqsir - Men shave their heads (Halq) or trim their hair (Taqsir). Women trim a small portion of their hair.
  • 9 Farewell Tawaf - Perform a final Tawaf around the Kaaba before leaving Mecca.
Important Notes
  • Hajj is obligatory once in a lifetime for Muslims who are physically and financially able
  • Umrah can be performed at any time of the year
  • Consult with a knowledgeable scholar or guide before performing Hajj
  • Ensure all necessary documents and vaccinations are completed

About Zakat

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam. It is obligatory for Muslims who meet the Nisab threshold (minimum wealth) to pay 2.5% of their wealth annually. Zakat purifies wealth and helps those in need.

Nisab: The minimum amount of wealth one must have before Zakat becomes obligatory. It is equivalent to 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver.
